Market Signal: From Talking to Doing

Over the past week, personal AI agents have crossed a visible threshold: mainstream assistants are no longer limited to conversation. Google’s Gemini now operates parts of the computer and browser environment, while Apple unveiled a next-generation Siri positioned as more proactive and context-aware at WWDC 2026. Meanwhile, Grok 4.5 entered private beta inside SpaceX and Tesla, underscoring how cutting-edge agents remain gated.

Demand is accelerating globally. An Adobe-backed report cited by BW Businessworld notes that 60% of Indian consumers want personal AI agents — the highest share in Asia Pacific. At the same time, eMarketer highlights how AI assistants are reshaping demand capture and search behavior.

Gemini

Gemini’s expansion into computer use and screen analysis signals Google’s intent to turn its assistant into an acting agent. Rollouts across Chrome, Android Automotive, and broader computer-use APIs show scale — but also raise security and control concerns.

Grok

Grok 4.5 remains largely inaccessible, with private beta testing at SpaceX and Tesla and new voice APIs landing on Vercel’s AI Gateway. For most buyers, Grok is still aspirational rather than deployable.

Siri

Apple’s next-generation Siri shows meaningful improvement, particularly in CarPlay and system-level contexts. However, it remains tightly bound to Apple’s ecosystem rather than open-ended computer workflows.

ChatGPT

ChatGPT continues to dominate as a general-purpose AI and agent framework, with users experimenting with multi-agent setups. Its strength is flexibility — not persistent, cached computer actions.

Folk & Orchids

Folk and Orchids represent a growing class of vertical and relationship-centric AI tools. They provide useful context for how agents are embedding into workflows, though neither is driving breaking news this cycle.
